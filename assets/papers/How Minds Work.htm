How Minds Work:

Human Minds, Animal Minds, Artificial Minds

 

 Course:                   UNHP 4453 Special Topics

Class meetings:    Tuesday & Thursday, 2:40 to 4:05

        Classroom:             FedEx Institute of Technology

                                         room 324

Instructor:                Stan Franklin
 

 

Will provide a conceptual and computational framework  for understanding how minds work in humans, in animals and in machines .

For upper division students from
•  computer science (interested in artificial intelligence)
•  biology (interested in cognitive ethology)
•  psychology (interested in cognitive psychology)
•  related fields (anthropology, sociology, philosophy, etc)

Non-honors students please contact the Honors Program Office for permission to register.
