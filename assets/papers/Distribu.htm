DISTRIBUTION BACKGROUND PERTAINING TO USE OF

INTELLIGENT AGENTS

 

The five principal "participants" in the Navy distribution process are the Sailor, the command, the Manning Control Authorities, the School Houses and the Navy (generally considered the Bureau of Naval Personnel or BUPERS). Each of the participants have concerns and needs that are delineated below (highest priority first):

 

The Sailor: (These are mostly in the variable called Duty Preference) ~ 400,000 Sailors

 

Individual desires

Where the Sailor wants to be (or doesn’t want to be)

See the world or put down roots

Shore Duty vs Sea Duty in most cases

Know as far in advance of the move as possible

Family considerations

Spouse/children stability, jobs, schools, etc.

Move when it is good for the family (Summer, kids not in school)

Safety

Economic reality

Second income often necessity (spouse)

Affordability of adequate housing

Promotion opportunity

Educational opportunities

 

The Command: ~250 ships and a few thousand other commands

The Command staffed to perform mission

Getting the person on board when needed

Before the pre deployment training

Before the departing Sailor has left (Not always)

The person has the required training

The person is of the appropriate grade level (Experience)

Maintain high morale of assigned Sailors

Includes family support

Ensure Sailors are promotable

Reward best performers

Training

 

The Manning Control Authority: ~ #3 Atlantic Forces, Pacific Forces & rest

 

Fleet readiness (All the Commands staffed to perform mission)

Deliver "war-ready" units to the warfighting Commanders-in-Chief

Minimize unfilled jobs

 

School Houses: There are many but I can’t give you a decent estimate.

 

Knowing the Class requirements as far in advance as possible

Level loading of classes

The Navy (BUPERS): Only 1 Navy but it executed by a person called a detailer and there are ~`200 detailers. Each working with a different skill & experience group)

 

Fleet readiness (Balance the staffing needs between, Atlantic, Pacific & Rest)

Personnel end-strength management

Keeping the authorized & budgeted number of Sailors in the Navy

(Congressional limit that is bad to go above or below)

Management of Permanent Change of Station funds (Minimize the Move Budget)

Minimize the need for training (Reutilization of trained Sailors)

Retention management

Treating people well so they don’t quit
